Enrollment

Mission Bit offers a two year course track that exposes high school students to multiple coding languages. We design our classrooms with an equity lens with a focus on building community and rigorous learning objectives that focus on building projects. Fall and Spring courses runs for 13 weeks, 4 hours a week. During the summer, we run intensive courses that last four weeks introduction courses and six week courses for advance students. Each term students take a field trip to a tech company, receive career and college advising related to the technology field and showcase their projects to a large community of supporters during our semester ending Demo Day event.

There are no GPA requirements any student that attends a San Francisco public school (including charter schools) is encouraged to apply. Given the lack of diversity in the tech industry and our focus on equity, we are reaching out to African American, Latino and female students that attend the following private schools: Archbishop Riordan, ICA, St. Ignatius, Mercy and Sacred Heart.

For Spring 2016 Courses:

Students that complete a semester with Mission Bit will be eligible for an opportunity to attend Hack Reactor's prep course for free and receive financing to cover the cost of tuition.
All 2016 spring semester students will take a tour of a SF Bay Area tech company.
All 2016 spring high school classes go from January 25th 2016 and end April 14th 2016.
Consistent attendance is required, students are not allowed to miss more than 3 classes. The last day of enrollment is February 4th.
Mandatory: All students/families will need to attend accepted student orientation Saturday January 23rd 2016 from 3pm to 5pm at 699 Townsend. Students must be accompanied at orientation by a parent or guardian.
Demo Day: Each semester ends with Demo Day, which is a mandatory component of classes participation.

For additional questions, you can contact Mission Bit CEO Stevon Cook at stevon@missionbit.com.

Intro Web Programming

This is our beginners course. In the Intro Web Programming class, students will learn HTML5, CSS3 and Javascript and some core programming concepts through project based instruction. Within the semester the class will build websites and interactive applications. At the end of the semester, the class is broken up into teams, each paired with an instructor and spends a few weeks building a final project.

Meets: Monday and Wednesday, 4pm - 6pm.
